Housekeeping and Safe Pathways

Clean, organized shops make better parts faster. Chips and spills hide hazards and wreck surface finish. Treat housekeeping as part of making a part, not an afterthought.

Chip and coolant management

During the cut

Use appropriate chip‑breaking toolpaths and flood or air as specified. Never reach into a running machine to clear chips.

After the cut

Sweep chips with a brush, use a chip hook for stringers, and absorb coolant with pads. Keep electronics and ways clean.

Shadow boards and tool audits

Shadow boards and labeled drawers prevent lost tools and speed setup. A 30‑second visual audit at the end of class catches missing items before they disappear.

Pathways and staging

Mark walkways with tape or paint and keep carts parked in staging boxes, not aisles. Clear floors prevent trips when carrying stock or moving vises.
