Metrology and Quality

If you can’t measure it, you can’t make it repeatably. Good measurements are consistent, documented, and tied to the drawing.

Instrument basics

Calipers

Versatile but lower resolution. Zero before use, apply gentle pressure, and measure twice from different orientations.

Micrometers

Higher resolution for critical features. Use the ratchet stop for consistent torque. Keep standards clean.

Tolerance and finish

Read drawing tolerances carefully. A part can be in‑tolerance and still perform poorly if surface finish is rough — match finish to function.

Inspection plans

List features to check, instruments to use, and acceptable ranges. Record nominal, tolerance, and actual measurements. If a dimension drifts, adjust your process, not the numbers.
