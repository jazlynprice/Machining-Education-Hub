Intro to CNC Milling — Deep Dive

Connect coordinate systems, workholding, and chip load into a safe first cut.

Back to Learn Open videos on YouTube

Coordinates and offsets

Machine coordinates define the home of the machine. Work coordinates (G54–G59) place your part inside that space. Touch off tools and set your work offset to match the CAM plan.

Workholding choices

Vise with parallels for rectangular stock; strap clamps for odd shapes; soft jaws for repeat work. Always prove clearance in CAM and dry run.

Feeds, speeds, chip load

Pick spindle speed and feed per tooth based on cutter diameter, material, and flute count. Use a calculator to start, then tune by sound and chip color/shape.

Safe prove‑out

Air‑cut above stock, single‑block, and keep a hand on feed hold. Stop the process the moment something looks off.
