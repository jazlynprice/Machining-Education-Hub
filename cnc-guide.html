Coordinates and offsets

Machine coordinates define the home of the machine. Work coordinates (G54–G59) place your part inside that space. Touch off tools to a known reference and set your work offset to the stock corner or center as planned in CAM.

Workholding choices

Vise with parallels

Great for rectangular stock. Keep at least 3 mm grip and deburr edges for better clamping.

Clamps and soft jaws

Use strap clamps for odd shapes or make soft jaws to fit. Always prove clearance in CAM and dry run.

Feeds, speeds, and chip load

Choose spindle speed and feed per tooth based on cutter diameter, material, and flute count. Use a calculator as a starting point, then listen for stable cutting and check chips. Blue chips or smoke mean adjust immediately.

Safe prove‑out

Single‑block, air‑cut above stock, and verify with dye or scribe. Keep a clear plan for stopping if anything looks wrong.
