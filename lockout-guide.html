Lockout / Tagout Basics

Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) prevents unexpected startup or energy release during maintenance and setup. It is a disciplined, step‑by‑step process — shortcuts can injure people instantly.

Identify all energy sources

List electrical, pneumatic, hydraulic, mechanical, and gravity energy. Many machines have more than one source — for example, the spindle motor (electrical) and a pneumatic drawbar. Document each point.

Apply locks and tags

Device selection

Use lockable disconnects, plug lockouts, valve covers, or hasps as needed. Each worker applies a personal lock and a tag with name, date, and contact.

Group lockout

When multiple people work on a machine, use a group hasp or lockbox. Every person adds their own lock — no shared keys.

Verify zero energy state

After locking out, attempt a start or try to move actuators to confirm nothing can energize. Bleed off stored air or hydraulic pressure. Only then is it safe to begin work.

Restoring to service

Remove tools and guards from the work zone, notify nearby people, and remove personal locks yourself. Never remove someone else’s lock without a formal process.
