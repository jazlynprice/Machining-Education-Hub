PPE Essentials

PPE is the last barrier after hazards are reduced by design and process. Chips, coolant, and noise never go to zero.

Back to Learn Open videos on YouTube

Eye and face protection

Safety glasses protect against impact; face shields add splash and grinding protection but never replace glasses. Fit them close without gaps and replace scratched lenses that distort vision.

  • Clear lenses indoors; shaded for outdoor work.
  • Anti‑fog helps around coolant.
  • Store in a case to avoid scratches.

Hearing protection

Earmuffs give quick, consistent protection. Earplugs work great when rolled and inserted properly. Pick devices that deliver enough reduction for your machine’s noise levels, and wear them during setup and cutting.

Hands and feet

Use cut‑resistant gloves for handling raw stock and swarf, but remove gloves near rotating tools. Wear closed‑toe or safety‑toe shoes with slip‑resistant soles. Tie back hair and avoid loose clothing.

Common mistakes

  • Gloves near spindles or live chucks
  • Scratched glasses that reduce visibility
  • Forgetting hearing protection during dry runs
