Machine Startup and Shutdown

Checklists reduce mistakes when attention is split between CAD/CAM, fixturing, and measurement. A consistent routine protects people and equipment.

Startup checklist explained

Before power‑on

Check guards, e‑stop, lubrication, air, and coolant. Verify workholding and tool condition. Confirm program, WCS, and offsets are correct.

First motion

Use single‑block and reduced feed. Keep a hand near feed hold. Watch the first approach move and top‑of‑stock touch‑off like a hawk.

Shutdown checklist explained

Return tools to holders, power down, wipe chips, and mop spills. Reset vises and sweep the table. Leave the machine ready for the next operator.

Emergency stops and recovery

Practice e‑stop and safe recovery with your instructor. After a stop, inspect tool, holder, and workholding before resuming. Never rush a restart.
